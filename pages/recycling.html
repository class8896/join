Recycling & Waste — Simple Guide

Follow local rules and keep streams clean to improve outcomes.

Sorting Basics

Germany uses the "Green Dot" (Der Grüne Punkt) system. This symbol on packaging indicates the manufacturer contributes to the cost of recovery and recycling.

  • Yellow Bin/Sack (Gelber Sack): Packaging made of plastic, metal, or composites (often with the Green Dot).
  • Blue Bin (Papier): Paper and cardboard.
  • Glass: Sorted by color (white, green, brown) at bottle banks.

Deposit Return Scheme (Pfand)

Germany has a mandatory deposit system for beverage packaging. Look for the Pfand logo.

  • Einwegpfand (Single-use): 0.25€ deposit. Plastic bottles and cans with the DPG logo. Return to reverse vending machines.
  • Mehrwegpfand (Reusable): 0.08€ or 0.15€ deposit. Glass or sturdy plastic bottles. Washed and refilled.

Organics & Compost

Food scraps can become compost where supported.

  • Use community programs where available
  • Avoid contamination

Hard-to-Recycle Items

Some items need special drop-off points.

  • Store safely until drop-off
  • Follow label instructions

Reduce & Reuse

Recycling is one step; reduction and reuse often have greater impact.

  • Buy durable goods
  • Repair when possible

FAQs

Why rinse containers?

To reduce contamination and odours in recycling bins.

Can soft plastics be recycled?

Check local programs; availability varies.

What about e-waste?

Use designated collection points for electronics.
